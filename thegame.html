Lasertag as a sport

Lasertag as a sport has been growing a lot in recent years and we keep adding new countries to the list of active participants at international tournaments. The best players in the game have been competing, in some cases, for decades and have thousands of hours of practice put into the game. The gameplay itself has grown organically since the mid 90s with a lot of development of various playstyles in different eras. Every lasertag player is unique and there is no given physical type which is considered optimal for the sport. At very top level lasertag does require very good physique as it is a very fast paced game, however, player intelligence is definitely the key factor which makes for a good competetive lasertag player.

At Zone031 you will see several players who are among the best in the world and have dedicated years of training to not only learn, but also figure out how to play the game. All new players are heavily encouraged to find their own playstyle, watch, adapt and change things that better players do in order to further enhance the game and grow the sport. You will meet an array of different player types as well as players with very different levels of experience.

Game formats

Team Competition

Team Competition is a game format standardised to a three team game. This means that three teams play against each other at the same time. Each team has their own base to defend while they try to crack the opposing teams' bases. Playing three teams at the same time gives the format a unique feel and dynamic and makes it a tactically very challenging game. The standard number of players per team is five, but four player tournaments are not uncommon, six was the former standard and other numbers are possible as well though very rare.

Team Elimination

Team Elimation is typically played with four teams but can be played with any number of teams with any number of team members. Players start with what is usually 10 lives and need to conserve these whilst scoring points and shooting away lives from opponents. If a player loses all their lives they are eliminated from the game and their score is reduced to 0 points. So do not lose all of your lives!

Baserun

Baserun is a fast-paced format played three players in two teams on a neutral base. The target of the game is as usual to outscore the other teams. In baserun it is very rare to see a team win against another team by not scoring more bases. This format is very intense in terms of movement and positioning (and respositioning) and is usually well liked by new players.

Solos, doubles and triples

The number of different types of games in lasertag are virtually endless. Various game formats of baserun, elimination, open scoring, and more are usually played as side events at major tournaments.

Rules

The complete rules span a lot of pages and really only makes complete sense for experienced players. The following are a simplification of the rules to allow inexperienced players a rough guide and the curious a general idea of how competetive lasertag is played.

No Running

You are not allowed to move with speed which exceeds your own control. As lasertag is played in a dark arena with a lot of walls it is unsafe to move at really high speeds as you may easily crash into other players, risking injury to both them and yourself. Players must at all times maintain a speed at which they are able to stop with no more than one step.

No Jumping

You are not allowed to jump either as a way of avoiding shots or reaching over high walls. This is again for everybody's safety as you are not in complete control as you are in the air and easily may obstruct other players from moving freely.

No Intentional Covering

You are not allowed to use your arms, any other part of your body or any clothing or other things to cover part of your pack. In order for lasertag to be a competetive sport, the packs need to be hitable while they are in play. It just makes the game more fun and interesting that way.

Give Way to Active Players

Lasertag is a very active sport but players can either be active or inactive depending on the status of one's pack. As you are waiting to be reactivated you need to make sure that you do not hinder an active player from progressing in the arena. Your time as an inactive player is best spent looking for a strong position to respawn.

No Climbing or Otherwise Improper Use of Arena

Do not climb on walls or boxes. Lasertag is played on the ground. Also do not pull at walls or misuse the arena in any other obvious way.
